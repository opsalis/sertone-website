Delivery Methods

Five Ways to Receive Your Data

When an API server responds through the Sertone Global Network, you choose how that response reaches you. Enable one method or all five.

Direct encrypted Cloud storage Blockchain relay Webhook callback Email notification

You control how you receive your data. When you register as a consumer, you choose which delivery methods to enable in your Sertone web console. You can enable one method or all five — Sertone tries them in order of speed until your data is delivered.

🔒
① Direct encrypted delivery ~50ms

The fastest path. Your response arrives in ~50 ms, encrypted end-to-end through the Sertone Global Network. No third party sees your data.

☁️
② Cloud storage ~200ms

Bring your own S3 bucket, MinIO instance, or Backblaze B2 account. Sertone encrypts the response and uploads it to your storage. You control the data, you control the retention. Perfect for audit trails and compliance.

🔗
③ Blockchain relay ~3s

The response is stored via backup relay, encrypted. Permanent, censorship-proof, tamper-evident. Even if every server in the world goes offline, your data is preserved. Takes ~3 seconds but provides the strongest delivery guarantee.

⚡
④ Webhook callback async

Sertone sends the response to a URL you specify. Great for async workflows, event-driven architectures, and integrations with tools like Zapier or n8n.

📧
⑤ Email notification async

Receive a notification or the actual data payload via email. Useful for alerts, monitoring, and low-frequency API calls.

The Sertone Global Network uses a proprietary fully encrypted protocol. All traffic is end-to-end encrypted regardless of which delivery method you choose. Payment is automatic — you only pay for successful deliveries.
