Pay Per Call, Not Per Month

Use a service once? Pay once. Use it a million times? Pay a million times. No tiers, no minimum spend. Subscriptions also available for unlimited access.

The Basics

How Per-Call Pricing Works

It is simple: every time you call a service, you pay the price of that call. Nothing more.

1

Load Your Balance

Add USDC (digital dollars) to your balance. Always worth $1.00. Buy it on Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, or any exchange.

2

Call Any Service

Browse the catalog, pick a service, and call it. Each call deducts the service price from your balance automatically.

3

Withdraw Anytime

Done? Withdraw your remaining balance at any time. No waiting period, no exit fees. Your money is always yours.

Why It Matters

Why Per-Call Is Better

Subscriptions are designed for the provider, not for you. Per-call pricing is fair for everyone.

No Waste

With a subscription, you pay the same whether you make 10 calls or 10,000. With per-call pricing, you pay exactly for what you use.

No Commitment

No annual contracts, no monthly minimums, no cancellation fees. Try a service with one call. If you like it, keep using it.

No Overpaying

Subscription tiers force you to guess your usage. Guess too low and you hit limits. Guess too high and you overpay. Per-call eliminates that.

No Surprise Bills

You prepay by loading a balance, so your spending is always under control. You cannot accidentally run up a bill you cannot afford.

The Math

What Makes Up the Cost

Every call has two parts. Both are visible upfront.

You pay the owner's price. Nothing more.

Service price — Set by the owner. This is what you pay per call.

5% platform fee — Deducted from the owner's side. The owner receives 95% of their stated price.

Example: A service priced at $0.10 per call costs you $0.10. The owner receives $0.095.

For Service Owners

Set Your Price, Keep 95%

The 5% fee is deducted from the total. You receive 95% of your stated price.

You set your service price to $0.50 per call.

A consumer calls your service 1,000 times today.

The consumer pays: 1,000 × $0.50 = $500.00

You receive: $500.00 × 95% = $475.00
